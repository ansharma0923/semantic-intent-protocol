# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Semantic Intent Protocol (SIP) project is committed to maintaining a
welcoming, respectful, and productive environment for all contributors,
regardless of background, experience level, or identity.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants in this project are expected to:

- Communicate professionally and respectfully in all project spaces,
  including issues, pull requests, discussions, and other forums.
- Provide and accept constructive feedback graciously.
- Focus discussions on technical merit and the goals of the project.
- Respect differing viewpoints and acknowledge that reasonable people
  may disagree on technical and design decisions.
- Give credit to others' contributions and ideas.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory remarks of any kind.
- Deliberate intimidation, trolling, or sustained disruptive behavior.
- Publishing private information about others without explicit permission.
- Any conduct that would be considered unprofessional or inappropriate
  in a technical work environment.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for upholding this Code of Conduct.
Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
contributions, comments, and other interactions that violate this standard,
and to temporarily or permanently restrict participation by individuals who
engage in unacceptable behavior.

Enforcement decisions will be made in good faith and applied consistently.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct,
please report it to the project maintainers privately. Reports will be
reviewed promptly and handled with discretion. All reports will be treated
confidentially.

See [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) for reporting security-specific concerns.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces managed by the SIP
project, and also applies when an individual is representing the project
in public spaces.
